6 National Parks in
France
1.Parque Nacional de Mercantour

With snowcapped Alps and a red-rock valley, France's Mercantour National Park is a biodiversity hot spot. Horned chamois graze the slopes of vertiginous mountains, marmots scurry among boulders and alpine violets, and the rare bearded vulture—Europe's largest raptor—wheels overhead.

  • Declared as a National Park on: 1979
  • Best time to visit: May-October
  • Famous for: Mountains.
  • Location: 23 Rue d'Italie, 06000 Nice, France
2.Parc national de forêts

A hotspot of biodiversity conservation in the heart of France, the Forêts National Park offers opportunities to visitors who venture there to make remarkable discoveries where nature, well-being, culture and history blend with the daily lives of the people that live in these lands.

  • Declared as a National Park on 2019
  • Best time to visit: September-November
  • Famous for: only French national park dedicated to forests and their inhabitants.
  • Location: 20 Rue Anatole Gabeur, 52210 Arc-en-Barrois, France
3.Écrins National Park

A high-mountain destination that is sunny throughout the year, the Ecrins are best revealed to the visitor on foot. There are more than 700 km of waymarked paths for you to explore the steep peaks, glaciers, immense alpine meadows, high-altitude lakes and capricious mountain streams. It’s the opportunity to discover sumptuous landscapes and exceptional biodiversitý, with more than 4000 identified animal and plant species.

  • Declared as a National Park on: 1973
  • Best time to visit: July-september
  • Famous for: Providing Avenues For Outdoor Activities.
  • Location: Domaine de Charance, 05000 Gap, France
4.Port-Cros National Park

A wild setting with preserved biodiversity, Port-Cros is one of ten French national parks. It is also the oldest marine park in Europe, occupying 1700 hectares of sunken land and 2900 hectares of sea. The island is also the cradle of a historic and cultural heritage. Consequently, you can find roman remains, some twenty military forts and around thirty shipwrecks that attract a multitude of sub-aqua enthusiasts. Multiple actions have also been implemented to preserve this rare site.

  • Declared as a National Park on: 1963
  • Best time to visit: September-December
  • Famous for: Hiking, Clean, Non-Commercialized Beaches
  • Location: 83400 Hyères, France
5.Vanoise National Park

The Vanoise National Park is a protected area, mostly on the territory of Haute Maurienne Vanoise, founded in 1963 thanks to Gilbert André, then mayor of Bonneval sur Arc. The central zone, called "heart", is a totally protected area with regulations that promote the biodiversity of landscapes, environments, species and preserve the cultural and mountain heritage.

  • Declared as a National Park on 1963
  • Best time to visit: October-November
  • Famous for: Glaciers, Valleys, Fauna and Flora.
  • Location: Parc national de la Vanoise, 73000 Vanoise, France
6.Guiana Amazonian Park

An ideal mythical land, within the Guiana Amazonian Park lies a strong imagination linked to its thriving nature and dense forests. According to recent studies, between eight and ten million people lived in the Amazon before the arrival of European explorers. This significant occupation has left many traces, although most of them have been covered over time by vegetation.

  • Declared as a National Park on 1978
  • Best time to visit: August-September and February-March
  • Famous For: Two Great Rivers
  • Location: 1 rue Lederson, Remire-Montjoly, French Guiana
