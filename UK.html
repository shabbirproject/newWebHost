6 National Parks in United
Kingdom
1. THE BROADS NATIONAL PARK

The Norfolk and Suffolk Broads are Britain’s largest protected wetland and are unlike anywhere else. A rich tapestry of landscape, water and wildlife; woven together by history.

  • Declared as a National Park on 1989
  • Best time to visit: September, April
  • Famous For: Swallowtail butterfly and Norfolk hawker dragonfly
  • Location: Norfolk
2. SOUTH DOWNS NATIONAL PARK

Just a short journey from London, the South Downs is known as the ‘gateway to England’ – its rolling green hills, globally important chalk grassland, ancient woodland, dark night skies, wildlife-rich heathland and windswept cliffs represents people and nature at their best.

  • Declared as a National Park on 2010
  • Best time to visit: June
  • Famous for: Adonis blue butterfly
  • Location: England
3. LAKE DISTRICT NATIONAL PARK

Step into the National Park and UNESCO World Heritage Site to discover spectacular landscapes, pretty villages, a warm welcome and a rich cultural heritage. Explore the fells, splash about on the lakes, take a wild adventure or simply enjoy the peace. Breathe in and be inspired.

  • Declared as a National Park on: 1975
  • Best time to visit: July–August
  • Famous for: Mountains
  • Location: Cumbria, England
4. PEMBROKESHIRE COAST NATIONAL PARK

It’s one of the smallest UK National Parks, but has one of the most diverse landscapes, covering almost all the Pembrokeshire Coast, every offshore island, the Daugleddau estuary and large areas of the Preseli Hills and the Gwaun Valley.

  • Declared as a National Park on: 1952
  • Best time to visit: March-May
  • Famous for: Beaches, cliffs, islands and wildlife
  • Location: Wales
5. NORTH YORK MOORS NATIONAL PARK

Sweeping moorland, a crinkly coast, ancient woods and timeless villages – the North York Moors is a special place, forged by nature and shaped over generations. Give yourself time to discover its secrets, from amazing wildlife to dazzling night skies.

  • Declared as a National Park on 1952
  • Best time to visit: September, May, February & March.
  • Famous for: Tiger, Nesting birds, Burgundy butterfly
  • Location: Yorkshire
6.Peak District

Sandwiched between Manchester and Sheffield, England’s oldest national park has two personalities: a tough and a gentle side. In the north are the Dark Peak’s wind-whipped moorland and dramatic edges like Kinder Scout – the Peak District’s highest plateau – which rewards adventurous walkers with stark views. Venture south for the lower, greener, meadow-covered dales of the White Peak. Walk along the Ecclesbourne Valley to experience both in one day.

  • Declared as a National Park on: 1951
  • Best time to visit: June-August
  • Famous for: mountains,largest lakes
  • Location: Keswick
